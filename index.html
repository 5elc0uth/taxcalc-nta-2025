What is Payroll Manager? ▾

The Payroll Manager is for employers, HR teams, and payroll bureaus who need to process PAYE tax for multiple employees at once — rather than calculating one person at a time.

Here's how to use it:

  1. Give the run a name — e.g. "March 2025 Payroll" — and a pay period.
  2. Add each employee with their salary components (basic, housing, transport, etc). Each employee's full NTA 2025 deductions and PAYE are calculated instantly.
  3. Review the table — see every employee's pension, NHF, NHIS, PAYE, and net take-home side by side.
  4. Check the summary bar — shows total gross payroll, the exact amount to remit to FIRS as PAYE, and total net to pay staff.
  5. Export or save — download as Excel for your records, print a PDF payroll computation sheet, or save the run to your private history.

All calculations follow the Nigeria Tax Act 2025. For compliance, consult a certified tax professional.

Business Tax (CIT)

Business Owners & Accountants
⌄
Auto-classify your company as Small, Medium, or Large — and see your CIT + Development Levy instantly.

Company Income Tax is what Nigerian companies pay on profit — separate from PAYE which employees pay. Under NTA 2025, companies are classified into three tiers based on turnover and assets, each with a different rate.

1Go to Business → Business Tax
2Enter your company name, period (quarterly or full year), annual turnover, assessable profit, and total assets
3TaxCalc automatically classifies your company: Small (0% CIT), Medium (20%), or Large (30%)
4See CIT payable, Development Levy (4%), total tax, net profit after tax, and effective rate
Small companies = turnover under ₦50m AND assets ≤ ₦250m → pay 0% CIT and are exempt from the Development Levy. The 4% Development Levy under NTA 2025 replaces four separate levies that previously existed (NITDA, NASENI, Education Tax, Police Trust Fund).

VAT Calculator

Business Owners & Accountants
⌄
Output minus input VAT = what you remit to FIRS. Zero-rated? TaxCalc flags it automatically.

Value Added Tax in Nigeria is charged at 7.5% on taxable goods and services. Businesses collect VAT from customers (output VAT) and can reclaim VAT they paid on business purchases (input VAT). You remit the net difference to FIRS monthly.

1Go to Business → VAT
2Enter your total taxable sales for the period (excluding VAT)
3Enter any input VAT you can claim from business purchases
4If your business is zero-rated or exempt (food, medical, education, exports), tick the checkbox — no VAT is due
5See your net VAT payable and the due date (21st of the following month)
If your input VAT exceeds output VAT, the result is a VAT refund — TaxCalc flags this clearly so you know to file a refund claim with FIRS.

NTA 2025 March 2026
Must Read

What the Nigeria Tax Act 2025 Actually Means for Your Salary

The most important tax change in two decades — explained simply.

The NTA 2025 replaces the decades-old PITA. The first ₦800,000 of your chargeable income is now completely tax-free — up from ₦300,000. For most employees earning ₦2m–₦10m annually, this means meaningfully lower PAYE every month.

The new rent relief — up to ₦500,000 off your taxable income — is an additional benefit most employees aren't claiming because their employers haven't updated payroll yet. Use the TaxCalc comparison tool to see exactly how much you save.

Business TaxFeb 2026

CIT Tiers Explained: Small, Medium, or Large?

Small companies (under ₦50m turnover) pay zero CIT. Medium pay 20%. Large pay 30%. Plus the new 4% consolidated Development Levy.

Under NTA 2025, your CIT liability depends entirely on which tier you fall into. Small companies with turnover under ₦50m AND assets not more than ₦250m pay zero CIT and are fully exempt from the Development Levy — a significant advantage for SMEs. Medium companies pay 20% CIT plus 4% levy. Large pay 30% plus the levy. The 4% Development Levy consolidates what were previously four separate levies: NITDA (1%), NASENI (0.5%), Education Tax (2%), and Police Trust Fund (0.5%) — simplifying compliance considerably.

VATFeb 2026

VAT 2025: What You Collect, Keep, and Remit

Nigeria's VAT stays at 7.5%. Output minus input = what you remit to FIRS by the 21st of the following month.

As a VAT-registered business, you collect output VAT from customers on every taxable sale and can reclaim input VAT on business purchases. If input exceeds output in any month, you have a VAT credit to carry forward or claim as a refund. Zero-rated categories include basic food items, medical supplies, educational materials, exported goods, and baby products — businesses in these categories charge 0% and may still reclaim input VAT.

PayrollJan 2026

HR Guide: Updating Your Payroll for NTA 2025

Haven't updated payroll yet? You're likely over-deducting PAYE from every employee's salary right now.

Practical steps: update your payroll software's tax table, confirm the new CRA formula (20% of gross income + ₦200,000), implement rent relief for employees who provide rent receipts, and run the NTA 2025 vs PITA comparison for each employee to quantify the difference. TaxCalc's Payroll tab lets you do this for your entire team in one run.

AccountantsJan 2026

Accountant's Checklist: Every Client Under NTA 2025

A systematic review every accountant should run for individual and corporate clients this year.

For individual clients: verify CRA application, confirm rent relief documentation, check pension at 8% of basic + housing + transport. For corporate clients: confirm CIT tier, check if they now qualify as small company (the ₦250m assets threshold catches many), calculate the 4% consolidated levy, and prepare the combined CIT + VAT + levy summary for the board.

Personal FinanceDec 2025

Rent Relief: The Benefit Most Employees Are Missing

Up to ₦500,000 off your taxable income. That's a real saving of ₦90k–₦105k per year for most employees.

To claim it, your employer needs to include your annual rent in their payroll computation and you need to provide a rental agreement or receipts. Many employers have not yet implemented this — if yours hasn't, you may be entitled to a tax refund for months since NTA 2025 took effect. Enter your rent in TaxCalc to see your personal saving.

NTA 2025Mar 2026

Pension Under NTA 2025: What Changed and What Didn't

Your pension contribution is still 8% — but the base it's calculated on matters more than ever under the new tax rules.

Under NTA 2025, pension (8% of basic + housing + transport allowances) remains a pre-tax deduction — meaning it reduces your chargeable income before PAYE is computed. This is unchanged from PITA. What has changed is that the expanded zero-rate band and new rent relief interact with pension to create a compounding relief effect: for a mid-range earner, combining pension, CRA, and rent relief can reduce effective PAYE by 30–40% compared to the old regime. Always confirm your employer is computing pension on the correct base — not total gross — as this is one of the most common payroll errors TaxCalc flags.
